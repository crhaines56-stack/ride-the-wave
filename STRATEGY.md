# Ride the wAIve — Operations Manual
**Community AI Meetup · Northwest Austin · April 2026**

---

## Mission
*"To ensure our community rides the AI wave and is not crushed by it."*

A free, monthly community meetup for residents of Steiner Ranch, Lakeway, River Place, and Four Points. Its purpose: help neighbors understand and apply AI in their businesses and daily lives — practically, not theoretically.

**Ride the wAIve is its own brand.** It is community-first, no hard sell, no Forge Agency branding in the room. Chris is present as a neighbor and community leader — not as a salesman. Trust is the product.

---

## Goals
- Grow to 40–50 active members within 6 months
- Recruit a majority of small business owners
- Operate as a community-run event requiring minimal founder time (~1 hr/month)
- Serve as the primary trust-building pipeline for Forge Agency

---

## Meeting Format — 90 Minutes

| Time | Segment | Who Runs It |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:15 | Open Networking | Everyone |
| 0:15–0:20 | Welcome & Mission | Chris (5 min max) |
| 0:20–0:45 | Main AI Demo / Talk | Member volunteer speaker |
| 0:45–1:05 | What Are You Working On? | 3 member volunteers (2–3 min each) |
| 1:05–1:20 | Open Q&A / Group Problem Solving | Monthly Facilitator |
| 1:20–1:30 | Wrap Up + Next Speaker Sign-Up | Content Curator |

---

## Volunteer Roles

Recruit these four roles at or before the first meeting. Offer each volunteer a free 30-minute AI strategy session with Chris as a thank-you.

**Monthly Facilitator**
Runs the agenda from welcome through Q&A. Keeps sessions on time. Rotates every 3 months. Asks at the end: "Who wants to facilitate next month?"

**Content Curator**
Recruits the main speaker each month. Maintains a running list of potential speakers. Sends the speaker invitation (see Templates) at least 3 weeks out. Confirms topic and AV needs one week out.

**Venue Coordinator**
Secures a free or low-cost venue. Confirms capacity (40–60 seats), Wi-Fi, projector. Shares address and parking in the group chat one week before.

**Group Chat & Events Admin**
Manages the community WhatsApp or GroupMe. Posts reminders 7 days and 1 day before. Updates the event listing with venue, speaker, and topic. Welcomes new members.

---

## Venue Strategy

Rotate venues across the four neighborhoods. Always free or low-cost.

**Approved types:** Public libraries · Coworking spaces · Restaurant private rooms · Church/civic rooms · Real estate or professional offices

**Requirements:** 40+ seats · Reliable Wi-Fi · Projector or large screen · Free parking · 90-min window + 30-min setup/teardown

## Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 — Months 1–2: Seed to 15–20 Members**
- Post launch announcement on Steiner Ranch, River Place, Four Points, Lakeway Facebook and Nextdoor groups
- Post in Austin small business owner groups on Facebook and LinkedIn
- Ask every founding member to personally invite one person before the next meeting
- Google Form for RSVPs (free — skip Meetup.com)

**Phase 2 — Months 3–4: Referral Engine (20–35 Members)**
- "Bring a Business Owner" challenge — member who brings the most new business owners gets a spotlight slot
- Partner with local chambers: Lakeway Area Chamber, Austin NW Chamber
- Ask speakers to share the event with their professional network

**Phase 3 — Months 5+: Sustain to 40–50 Members**
- Word of mouth becomes the primary engine as members get real value
- Quarterly "guest meetup" with a well-known local AI practitioner as a growth spike
- Private community chat (WhatsApp or GroupMe) drives ongoing engagement between events

## Business Development — How This Feeds Forge Agency

Chris is positioned as a community leader, not a salesperson. Consulting and software opportunities emerge naturally from trust built over time.

**Structural touchpoints:**
- End every meeting with: *"If anything sparked an idea you want to explore, I'm happy to grab coffee."*
- Offer a free AI demo / audit as a door prize at each meeting — prioritize business owners when selecting the winner
- When members ask what you do, lead with the community — let Forge come up organically

**Pipeline flow:**
1. Member joins via Nextdoor / Facebook / referral
2. Attends 1–2 meetings, builds trust with Chris and community
3. Wins or is offered the door prize demo
4. Demo reveals specific opportunities in their business
5. Natural conversation moves toward a Forge Agency engagement

## Launch Checklist

**Week 1**
- [ ] Post launch announcement on Steiner Ranch, Four Points, River Place, Lakeway Facebook and Nextdoor
- [ ] Post in Austin small business Facebook and LinkedIn groups
- [ ] Text or email 10 people personally to seed the first meeting

**Week 2**
- [ ] Create WhatsApp or GroupMe group chat
- [ ] Set up Google Form for RSVPs
- [ ] Set recurring calendar event: second Tuesday of each month, 6:30–8:00 PM
- [ ] Book the first venue

**Meeting #1**
- [ ] Run the 90-minute agenda (Chris facilitates this one time only)
- [ ] At 75-minute mark, announce the four volunteer roles and ask for hands
- [ ] Collect names and contact info for each volunteer
- [ ] Announce the door prize (free AI demo) and select a winner
- [ ] End with: *"If anything sparked an idea, I'm happy to grab coffee."*

**After Meeting #1**
- [ ] Brief each volunteer on their role (30 min total, group call or coffee)
- [ ] Hand off group chat admin and venue coordination
- [ ] Let the community start running itself
